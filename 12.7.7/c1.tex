\textbf{Question 12.7.7:} 
 A charged $30 \mu F$ capacitor is connected to a $27 mH$ inductor. What is the angular frequency of free oscillations of the circuit?\\
\textbf{Solution: }
\begin{table}[h!]
    \centering
    \input{tables/table2.tex}
    \caption{Input Parameters}
    \label{tab:table_omega}
\end{table}
\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \input{figs/fig1.tex}
    \caption{LC Circuit Diagram}
    \label{fig:omega2}
\end{figure}

From Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL):
\begin{equation}
V(t) = V_L + V_C
\end{equation}
Using reactances from \figref{fig:2},
\begin{align}
    V(s) &= sL I(s) + \dfrac{1}{sC} I(s)\\
    &= I(s)\left( Ls + \dfrac{1}{sC}\right)\\
    \implies I(s) &= \dfrac{V(s)}{\left(Ls + \dfrac{1}{sC}\right)} 
   \end{align}
\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \input{figs/fig2.tex}
    \caption{LC Circuit Diagram}
    \label{fig:2}
\end{figure}
At resonance, the reactances of capacitor and inductor cancel out as follows:
\begin{align}
    Ls + \dfrac{1}{sC} &= 0\\
    \implies s &= j\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{LC}} 
\end{align}
$s$ can be expressed in terms of angular resonance frequency as
\begin{equation}
    s = j\omega_0 
\end{equation}
on comparing (6) and (7)
\begin{equation}
    \omega_0 = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{LC}}
\end{equation}
\begin{align}
\omega_0 &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{(30 \times 10^{-6}) \times (27 \times 10^{-3})}} \\
&= \frac{1}{\sqrt{8.1 \times 10^{-7}}}\\
& \approx 1.11 \times 10^{3} \, \text{rad/s}
\end{align}
